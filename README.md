# CCIHackathon
 Whisper — Crisis companion for teens

An AI that detects early signs of depression or suicidal ideation in journal entries or voice notes. It doesn't replace therapy — it gently nudges the teen to reach out to a trusted adult, and alerts a parent/counselor if risk is high. Built with consent and privacy first.

1. The Homepage (The Safe Haven)
The goal here is to build immediate trust. It shouldn't feel like a medical portal; it should feel like a supportive friend.

Hero Section: A calming, minimalist design with a clear value proposition: "A private space to be heard, a gentle hand to guide you."

How It Works: A 3-step visual guide (1. Share your thoughts via text or voice; 2. Whisper listens and understands; 3. Get insights and gentle nudges).

Immediate Help Button: A persistent, high-contrast "Need help right now?" button that links to 24/7 crisis hotlines.

Value Pillars: Brief blurbs on Privacy, Non-Judgmental AI, and the "Human-in-the-loop" philosophy.

2. "How We Listen" (The AI & Ethics Page)
Teens (and parents) are often skeptical of AI. This page explains the "brain" behind Whisper.

Detection vs. Diagnosis: A clear statement that the AI identifies patterns and emotions but does not provide medical diagnoses.

The Nudge System: Explain how the AI suggests talking to a counselor or parent when it detects a shift in mood.

Safety Thresholds: Transparency on what triggers an alert to a trusted adult, ensuring the teen knows exactly where the line of "total privacy" meets "emergency intervention."

3. The Teen Dashboard (The Product Preview)
This page shows the user what their daily experience will look like.

Journaling Interface: A preview of the voice-to-text and "Free-write" modes.

Mood Landscape: A visual representation of their emotional trends over time (e.g., a "galaxy" of moods rather than just a boring bar graph).

The "Pocket Resource" Library: Teasers of "Micro-coping" skills—30-second breathing exercises or grounding techniques.

4. "For the Circle" (The Parent & Counselor Portal)
This section is tailored to the adults who will receive the alerts.

Onboarding for Adults: How to set up the "Trusted Adult" profile and what the consent process looks like.

The Alert Protocol: A step-by-step guide on what to do when they receive a high-risk notification from Whisper.

Resource Kits: Guides on "How to start the conversation" if Whisper alerts them that their teen is struggling.

5. Privacy & Data Ethics (The "No-Spying" Guarantee)
Because privacy is the #1 concern for teens, this needs to be more than just a legal document.

Human-Readable Privacy: A simplified version of the Privacy Policy (e.g., "We don't sell your data," "Your entries are encrypted").

Consent Flow: A detailed look at how the teen grants permission for alerts to be sent to their parent/counselor.

Data Deletion: Clear instructions on how a user can wipe their history at any time.

6. The Crisis Resource Center
This should be a standalone, easily accessible directory.

Global/Local Hotlines: Click-to-call links for the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, Trevor Project, and Crisis Text Line.

Safety Planning: A digital template for a "Safety Plan" that users can fill out when they are in a headspace to do so.
